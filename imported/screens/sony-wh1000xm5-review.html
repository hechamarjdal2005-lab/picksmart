Our Verdict

The Sony WH-1000XM5 maintains the crown as the best all-around noise-canceling headphones for most people. While the new design trade-off for portability may annoy some travelers, the massive leap in active noise cancellation (ANC) and microphone performance makes these an essential upgrade for remote professionals and commuters alike.

Modernized Design & Comfort

Sony has completely overhauled the silhouette for the XM5. Gone are the chunky hinges and folding mechanics of the previous four generations. In its place is a sleek, "noiseless" design featuring a stepless slider and thinner headband. The synthetic leather earcups are incredibly soft, providing a friction-free seal that remarkably suppresses external wind noise even before the electronics kick in.

ANC Performance: The New Gold Standard

With two processors controlling eight microphones, the WH-1000XM5 doesn't just block sound—it anticipates it. In our testing, it excelled at removing the high-pitched chatter of a crowded cafe and the screeching of subway brakes—areas where almost all other ANC headphones struggle. The Auto NC Optimizer automatically adjusts the cancellation based on your surroundings and wearing conditions.

Battery Life & Smart Features

30 hours of battery life with ANC on is still plenty, though Sennheiser has pushed the bar higher recently. However, Sony wins on charging speed: a mere 3 minutes on a USB-PD charger gives you 3 full hours of playback. The Speak-to-Chat feature remains a highlight, instantly pausing your music and enabling transparency mode the moment you start talking.
